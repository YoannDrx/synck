Jean-Pierre Ménager:
Jean-Pierre Ménager is a renowned French musician, composer, arranger and conductor with a multifaceted, international career spanning more than 80 destinations. He has left his mark on classical music, jazz, chanson and theater.
He started young, encouraged by a diatonic accordion received at age four. After extensive classical studies—crowned with several first prizes and the prestigious “Prix ORTF de l’accordéon”—he expanded to piano and other instruments. He sailed the world as bandleader on the liner Le Mermoz, a flagship of the Compagnie Paquet, sharing his art with passengers from every horizon.
As a musician, Jean-Pierre has played and recorded with classical luminaries like Maurice André and James Galway, and French theater figures such as Michel Leeb and Jean-Claude Brialy. His passion for jazz led to collaborations with François Jeanneau and musicians from Jermaine Jackson’s and George Duke’s circles. In chanson, he accompanied legends including Charles Dumont and Isabelle Aubret.
A prolific composer and orchestrator, he has written more than 650 works in his custom-built studio, covering film music, dance, chanson, jazz and more. He has contributed to over 50 albums and countless radio jingles. His ability to embrace and innovate across styles is evident in his diverse catalog.
Recently he launched “Une certaine histoire de France en chansons”, a show that travels from the Paris Commune to the 1960s—highlighting his knack for linking history and music with evocative storytelling and melodies.
With a life devoted to music, Jean-Pierre Ménager remains an emblematic figure. His eclectic body of work and masterful performances continue to inspire listeners worldwide.
