Charlotte Savary:
Charlotte Savary is a renowned French singer-songwriter with a distinctive timbre marked by a light, fast vibrato. She explores pop, folk, electro, hip-hop and jazz, performing in both French and English.
From the outset she has shone in influential projects. She is best known for her close collaboration with Wax Tailor, contributing to every album since they met during the 2004 recording of “World’s End Lane” with Clover. She features on key tracks like “Our Dance”, “Seize the Day” and “The Games You Play”, and her lyrics are praised for their poetry and emotional storytelling.
Beyond Wax Tailor, Charlotte co-founded multiple projects. With Philippe Chevallier she formed the folk duo Felipecha, releasing “De fil en aiguille” (2008) and “Les Lignes de Fuite” (2011). She dazzled solo with the 2016 album “Seasons”, an intimate exploration of love and life’s cycles. In 2020 she launched SEYES with Marine Thibault, blending genres on the album “Beauty Dies”.
Her path also includes contributions to varied projects like Olivier Libaux’s Queens of the Stone Age covers and the ephemeral “Meeting Point”, a hybrid of Afro jazz, pop and electro.
Charlotte was a key member of Télépopmusik, enriching the group with her vocals and lyrics from 2004 to 2019. Her solo work continues to evolve with projects such as “Illuminations” and “Digger’s Daughter”, pairing acoustic sensitivity with captivating melodies.
Known for her ease with harmonies and ability to craft diverse sonic moods, Charlotte excels in the studio and on stage. She commands the stage with a presence that is both sensual and natural. Her home studio lets her finesse productions, underscoring her technical skill and artistic commitment.
With a career full of fruitful collaborations and poignant solo projects, Charlotte Savary remains an emblematic figure of contemporary French music. Her voice and compositions continue to reach audiences worldwide.
